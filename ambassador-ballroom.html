The Room

An address that announces the occasion before a single guest arrives.

Some rooms are booked. The Ambassador is inherited — passed from one unforgettable night to the next since the spring of 1924, when its doors first opened beneath a ceiling leafed in gold.

Beneath nine hand-blown chandeliers, the grand hall seats five hundred for dinner and holds twice that for the dance that follows. Every detail — the marble, the mirrorwork, the light — was made to be remembered.
